# LeadHunterOS — Acceptable Use & Data Subject Rights

## What data this system collects

LeadHunterOS collects **business-professional data only** from public sources:

- Name and job title (self-published on company websites, conference pages, ORCID, job boards)
- Company name, domain, and industry
- Work location (city/region level — not home address)
- Public profile URLs (LinkedIn search link, ORCID, Sessionize — not scraped content)
- Buying signals (job postings, funding filings, news mentions, HN/Reddit discussions)

**What is explicitly excluded at ingest (Layer 3 field minimization):**

- Home or personal address
- Personal phone number
- Political affiliation, religious belief, health/disability data
- Sexual orientation or gender identity
- Race or ethnicity
- Financial data beyond company funding round (public filings only)
- Government-issued ID numbers
- Biometrics
- Family or relationship data

## Data sources and legal basis

| Source | Type | Legal basis |
|---|---|---|
| SEC EDGAR Form D | US government public record | Public domain |
| ORCID profiles | CC0 open data (user-opted-in) | CC0 license |
| Sessionize/conference speakers | Self-published opt-in bios | Legitimate interest / public disclosure |
| Company "About/Team" pages | Company self-published | Legitimate interest |
| JobSpy (LinkedIn/Indeed/Google Jobs) | Public job postings, no login | Legitimate interest |
| HN, Reddit, GitHub, ProductHunt, RemoteOK | Public APIs, no auth | Legitimate interest |
| Google News RSS | Public RSS feed | Legitimate interest |
| Crunchbase News RSS | Public RSS feed | Legitimate interest |
| Wellfound public listings | Public job board | Legitimate interest |

**LinkedIn data is collected only via JobSpy job postings (no login, no profile scraping).
LinkedIn is one-of-N sources, not a primary data source.**

## Prohibited uses

The following uses of data generated by LeadHunterOS are prohibited:

- Stalking, harassment, or surveillance of individuals
- Discriminatory hiring, lending, insurance, or housing decisions
- Immigration enforcement targeting
- Generating inferences about protected characteristics
- Sending unsolicited personal communications to individuals who have opted out
- Re-selling raw contact data to third parties without additional compliance review
- Any use requiring individual consent that has not been obtained
- Training AI models on personal data without legal basis

## Your data rights (GDPR / CCPA)

If you believe data about you has been collected or processed by a system using
LeadHunterOS, you have the right to:

- **Access:** request what data is held about you
- **Correction:** request inaccurate data be corrected
- **Deletion:** request your data be deleted ("right to be forgotten")
- **Portability:** request your data in a machine-readable format
- **Objection:** object to processing based on legitimate interest

**To exercise any of these rights**, contact the operator of the system you
are interacting with. If you are using the open-source LeadHunterOS directly,
submit a request via the repository's issue tracker and your data will be
removed within 30 days.

## Opt-out

To opt out of data collection about you in any LeadHunterOS-powered system:

1. File an issue at the repository with the subject "Data Deletion Request"
2. Include your full name and any domains/companies associated with you
3. The operator must confirm deletion within 30 days

System operators deploying LeadHunterOS in production **must** implement:
- A public opt-out form reachable from their product's privacy policy
- A deletion pipeline that propagates to any warm caches or downstream exports
- A log of deletion requests and their completion dates

## Retention

Data collected by LeadHunterOS should be:
- Retained no longer than 24 months from the date of collection
- Re-verified against source before use after 90 days
- Deleted upon opt-out request within 30 days

## Operator responsibilities

If you deploy LeadHunterOS in a commercial product, you are responsible for:
- Adding a Privacy Policy that discloses data sources and collection practices
- Implementing the opt-out form and deletion pipeline described above
- Consulting legal counsel in your jurisdiction regarding GDPR, CCPA, and
  any local privacy regulations before processing data about individuals
- NOT representing that this system is equivalent to licensed data providers
  (PDL, Coresignal, Apollo, etc.) for compliance purposes — it is not
